# Website Research Notes

The EXHOLD site structure was informed by current official product pages from Tesla, Unitree Robotics, Boston Dynamics and 1X.

## Patterns Observed

- **Tesla Model Y:** Full-screen hero, short product promise, immediate order/demo CTAs, compact metric blocks, product variants and specifications.
- **Unitree shop/product pages:** Direct product pricing, add-to-cart or contact-sales flow, product versions, shipping/customs notices and technical feature sections.
- **Boston Dynamics:** Enterprise robotics positioning, product use cases, partner/customer trust, contact-sales CTA, training/support and deployment resources.
- **1X NEO:** Consumer-home robotics storytelling, subscription and ownership pricing, pre-order framing, FAQ, legal/privacy references and clear household-use imagery.

## Applied To EXHOLD

- Hero section uses a premium product render and two direct CTAs.
- Product sections explain benefits through real household scenarios, not abstract technology.
- Pricing section uses Malaysian Ringgit and separates purchase from rental.
- Safety section is deliberately prominent because EXHOLD is a concept assistive product.
- The form is a demo-only pilot-interest flow, suitable for a frontend-only Vercel project.
